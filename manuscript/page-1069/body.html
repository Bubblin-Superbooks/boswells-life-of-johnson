As Mr. Burke and Mr. Langton were walking home, Mr. Burke observed that Johnson had been very great that night; Mr. Langton joined in this, but added, he could have wished to hear more from another person; (plainly intimating that he meant Mr. Burke. "O, no (said Mr. Burke,) it is enough for me to have rung the bell to him."')

'Beauclerk having observed to him of one of their friends, that he was aukward at counting money, "Why, Sir, (said Johnson, I am likewise aukward at counting money. But then, Sir, the reason is plain; I have had very little money to count."')
